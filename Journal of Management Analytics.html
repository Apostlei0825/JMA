TitleThe impact of Danmaku on digital engagement of online video Platform
TimeFeb 15, 06:47 UTC
Overall evaluation
1: weak accept
The paper employs a difference-in-differences (DID) model to explore the effects of a newly introduced comment component on sponsored posts within the social media platform Bilibili. The results indicate that the adoption of the comment component significantly increases engagement with sponsored posts. Mediation effect of influencers’ effort shows that the adoption of the comment component encourages influencers to produce longer posts with more positive sentiment, which in turn enhances engagement. In addition, heterogeneous analysis and robustness checks have been tested.
I appreciate the authors’ effort in conducting the research. But I have several comments for the authors’ consideration in developing this paper. The detailed comments are provided below.
(comment 1) The paper shows the comment component for sponsored posts launched on June 6, 2022 in chapter 3.1, but the data you collected does not cover that time period. Please check this issue.
(comment 2) The current paper lacks clear a discussion of research motivation, which makes the research contribution insufficient. The literature proposed by the author do not provide a solid argument. The authors are suggested to spend more efforts in revealing the underlying research motivation.
(comment 3) I’m confused about introducing post character length and post sentiment score as mediating variables due to the lack of a sufficient theoretical framework, which may exist any other factors.
(comment 4) The arguments for hypotheses are not quite rigorous. The paper attempts to explain how comment component prompts the influencer to achieve longer posts with more positive sentiment to improve post engagement, but it does not explain the direct significance of comment component. In addition, the paper uses ‘influencers with higher followers put in less effort likely stems from an over-reliance on their established follower base’ to explain the negative moderating effect of ‘Number of Followers’, which is not fully convincing. In my intuition, influencers with more followers tend to gain greater benefits from sponsorship brands, which can motivate them to invest more effort and time in content creation. Considering the impact of sponsorship on the model might lead to better results.
(comment 5) It’s not clear about the posting frequency of influencers in the aim platform of the data. Perhaps using weekly data for model estimation would yield better results.
(comment 6) The conclusion is too brief and superficial built on the general discussion of the results to evaluate the novelty and uniqueness of this paper. The section of ‘Limitation and future research’ is also missing.

AbstractSocial media influencer marketing has grown substantially in the last decade and is a major advertising channel for many brands. Nowadays, social media platforms are taking embedded interactive traffic conversion tools (e.g., product tags in Instagram) to reduce the steps between sponsored posts and final purchases to improve conversion rates. However, given the simultaneous potential for increased conversion rates and user aversion to advertisements, it is unclear how the adoption of the novel tool affects sponsored post engagement. In this study, we explore an exogenous comment component launch on a popular social media platform in China (i.e., Bilibili). Our empirical results suggest that the adoption of the comment component increases sponsored post engagement, which is reflected in the increment of likes, favorites, and comments. We further explore the potential mechanism from the theoretical perspective of the motivation-opportunity-ability (MOA) framework and find that influencers get motivated endeavor to their posts with longer text and more positive emotions to catch attention. Our findings have novel and important implications for stakeholders of social e-commerce.
